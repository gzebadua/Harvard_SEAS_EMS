Where can I read the rules of each room?

There are several alternatives when you go to choose a room. There are rules common to all rooms and some rules specific to special rooms:

  • Common rules:
1. Due to academic scheduling, EMS allows you to reserve rooms up until May 31st 2013. 
 
2. Please note that academic programming takes priority. Academic classes, sections, weekly group meetings, thesis defenses, qualifying exams and colloquia will continue to be reserved and overseen by the SEAS Office of Student Affairs at classes@seas.harvard.edu. Contact: Tricia Jacome 
 
 
3. Room usage on weekends will incur charges for security/custodial services: Security/Custodial services: $40 per hour with a 4 hour minimum. 
 
4. Buildings Hours are Monday - Friday, 7 AM to 10 PM. 
 
 
  • Reserve a Room:
 
1. All common rules and:
 
2. You need to reserve the room at least 7 days prior to the event.
 
3. Please keep in mind that if you are serving food and need setup time to add a half hour to the beginning of your reservation. For larger events, you may require a longer setup period and some cleanup time. 
 
 
  • Express Reservation:
1. All common rules and:
 
2. Reservation date must be within the next 7 days.
 
3. No services nor special setups will be required for the reservation.
 
4. Please keep in mind that if you are serving food and need setup time to add a half hour to the beginning of your reservation. For larger events, you may require a longer setup period and some cleanup time. 
 
 
  • Dean's Conference Room:
1. All common rules and:
 
2. You need to reserve the room at least 7 days prior to the event.
 
3. Please note that on occasion, we may have to move your event due to the nature of the Dean's business.
 
 
  • Brooks Room:
1. All common rules and:
 
2. You need to reserve the room at least 7 days prior to the event.
 
3. To use the Brooks Room, a SEAS Faculty member must be present or sponsor the event. 
 
4. Administrative meetings are not permitted in the Brooks Room.
 
 
  • Pierce Hall 301:
1. All common rules and:
 
2. You need to reserve the room at least 7 days prior to the event.
 
3. This room is using a prioritization model to ensure the best use of this space: 1. SEAS Classes, 2. SEAS Sections, 3. Teaching Events, 4. Non-teaching events
 
4. One time non-SEAS events may not be scheduled more than three weeks in advance
 
5. One time non-SEAS events may be bumped to accommodate SEAS events, typically with one week’s notice.
 
6. Please clean up after yourselves and leave the room as you found it. Feel free to reconfigure the room to your needs, but please put the furniture back in its original arrangement before you leave (a diagram will be posted in the room to illustrate the set up). 
 
7. Please remove all course-related materials, all food and drink, trash, etc. from the room when you leave.
 
8. Please leave the whiteboards clean.
 
9. All course-related material ought to be stored in the lockers in the anteroom or in space other than the main classroom. Please discourage students from leaving projects (and/or materials related to projects) in Pierce 301 with "do not remove" signs. This room is occasionally used for events sponsored by the Dean (and sometimes by the President). We will alert all room users of such events.
 
10. Please take care to minimize damage to walls and furniture. If it is necessary to affix projects to walls and furniture, please use painters’ tape, which should mitigate such damage. Please ask students to cover any surfaces when they are using glue that is not water-based.
